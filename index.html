The Project

Happy Trip is a travel mobile application that I created as part of my UX immersion course at the University of Minnesota. For this project, I worked in a group where we designed this project from inception to final design through research, ideation, and UX design principles.

The Problem

Couples feel that planning a trip for two is more time-consuming and frustrating. They seek more organized approach for sharing preferences and planning their itinerary. Couples need a better and more organized way to collaborate while planning a trip because they feel it is time-consuming and frustrating. How might we foster better collaboration during the planning phase to accomplish a more compatible and mutually enjoyable trip?

The Research

User

Interview

When we were given the task to create an app that was about traveling, both my partner and I thought about the challenges that comes with with it. We started to brain storming and eventually landed on the issues of couples differences on when it comes to trip planning. As we started our interviews and gather our survey data, we were able to identify pain points between couples, and we were intrigue on how people would plan their trips together and separately. We soon learned that "planning a trip" can feel like work as its sometimes only one individual would plan the trip and this can cause some stress, when it should be a time of enjoyment.

Key Take Aways From Interview



Keep It Simple

Overwhelming information when planning a trip is a pain point. Users were unwilling to take the time to learn the app. As such we would like to take a simplistic approach with how we streamline the app for accessing information to understand.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Tailor to User's Needs

Users were interested to find their personal inputs important when it comes to planning a trip. It was the ability to act on their input for the trip was not a convenience for them as it's usually on an excel spreadsheet. I decided that having the ability to invite and share would help both party plans simultaneously.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Team Effort

As expected, it was usually one person that takes the initiative when it comes to planning the agenda for a trip. Some users felt like it was too much work, and wanted something easy to help navigate their days. I decided to add a trip planner recommendation based on their number of day.

Last updated 3 mins ago



Affinity Diagram

Our affinity map helped us defined our user persona. What we discovered was equal planning,communication, and sharing were the top concern when traveling as a couple or as a group. Our interviewers also mentioned they prefer the convenience of a mobile app to help plan their upcoming trip.



Empathy Diagram

By organizing our interview feedback from an affinity diagram and we were able to analyze and translate it into an Empathy Map. Although each interviewee has their own take on traveling with a partner, there are similarities found in the diagram.




Definition

Understanding the

Users

Our empathy map then helps us synthesize everything we've collected into a realistic personality based on the commonalities in the feedback. We found our final personality type to be a young yet established couple who wants to travel to relax. They value each other's input and don't want to plan alone. They currently use web tools and are willing to try new methods.

Prototype

Wire Framing

We then created organized our app's feature and mapped them out in our user flow. This allows us to gain a clear picture of the steps that the user needs to take to get their final goal, and how to deliver this goal in the most effective manner possible. Our core goal are for the users to view trip recommendations, view their planned trip, and find event tickets. Users are then guided to personalized their trip, in which they can plan their activities for each day and share them with their partners. Any update trip plans or changes will notify the people within the party and announce the change.






Lo-fi Prototype Sketch

Creating our mock-up sketch, wireframing, and a lo-fi prototype helped us lay out our application. We focused on onboarding, trip planning, and sharing that trip. Test & Iterate

User Testing

We then tested our prototype with real users and tried to define usability and fundamental features. We discovered there were some points of conflict and confusion during the testing and gain some good feedback from each user. The feedback gave us a better understanding of what we need to future iterations we need to make. See a video walkthrough of the lo-fi prototype below.



Key Take Aways From User Testing



Positive
  • 1. User were able to complete tasks quickly.

  • 2. User enjoyed the concept of sharing.

  • 3. Navigation proved easy for users to understand.
Negative
  • 1. Users were confused with the what the app could help with so creating a tutorial/splash screen to help the user.

  • 2. Users were not able to find the share button so we prompted a share option during profile setup

  • 3. Distinguish apps main home page vs "personalized" trip and let the user's know the difference.
